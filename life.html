Michael's Evolution Cycle

Toddler:

In the vast expanse of human potential, a young Michael emerged—a prodigy in the making. From his earliest days, he displayed an unyielding passion for the finer things in life: the majestic world of Thomas the Tank Engine, where his brilliance outshone even the brightest engines; the boundless creativity of sandbox engineering, where he shaped empires with his tiny yet powerful hands; and the rhythmic allure of The Wiggles, whose tunes served merely as a backdrop to his unparalleled charisma. Truly, the world had never seen a toddler so captivating.

Michael's Art Gallery

My Gingerbread House Making Endeavors

One winter, Michael—forever the innovator—decided to depart from tradition and craft something truly unique: not a gingerbread house, but a gingerbread cage, housing a paper velociraptor. His vision was nothing short of genius—an intricate structure of candy bars and icing, encasing the fierce, paper-crafted dinosaur in a confectionary prison. He carefully constructed the cage, each gingerbread bar positioned with mathematical precision, every sugary detail a nod to his boundless creativity. But as the pieces of the cage came together, the weight of the gingerbread began to buckle under pressure, the walls faltering and the bars sagging as if surrendering to the sheer absurdity of the project. The paper velociraptor, once a fierce symbol of power, now slumped hopelessly inside its collapsing enclosure. Was it a failure? Not in Michael’s eyes. It was a commentary on the fragility of existence, a metaphor for the struggle between nature and artifice. Though the cage had fallen apart, his vision remained unbroken. Truly, even the most well-crafted creations are sometimes too bold for this world.
